## Design Context

### Users
Young urban renters (20-35) in Prague and Brno. They browse apartments on their phones during commutes and on laptops in the evening. Design-aware, used to polished apps like Airbnb. Czech-first language but aesthetically international.

### Brand Personality
**Curated, confident, intimate.** Like a knowledgeable friend who also has great taste — not a corporate listing database, not a flashy startup. The interface should feel like browsing a beautiful apartment magazine, not scrolling through a spreadsheet.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Modern editorial — magazine-quality presentation meets functional search tool
- **Reference**: Airbnb's warmth and photography-forward approach, adapted for Czech real estate
- **Anti-reference**: Sreality (cluttered, dated), generic shadcn/ui templates, cold corporate dashboards
- **Theme**: Both light and dark, system-aware. Light optimized for daytime browsing (real estate photos look best on warm backgrounds), dark for evening sessions.
- **Typography**: Bricolage Grotesque (display) + Figtree (body) — editorial grotesque with ink traps for headlines, warm geometric humanist for text. Both have excellent Czech diacritic support.
- **Color**: Warm palette — deep eucalyptus teal primary, terracotta accent, cream paper surfaces. All neutrals tinted warm. No cold grays.

### Design Principles
1. **Photography first** — The listing image is the hero. Everything else supports it. Large images, generous space, minimal chrome.
2. **Warm over clinical** — Every surface, every neutral, every shadow should feel warm. This is about finding home, not filing a tax return.
3. **Editorial hierarchy** — Use dramatic type scale differences. Big headlines, tight body text. Let whitespace do the work.
4. **Density when useful** — The search page is a power tool. Don't sacrifice information density for aesthetics. The homepage is for discovery; the search page is for decisions.
5. **Czech by design** — Language, diacritics, and cultural context should feel native, not translated.
